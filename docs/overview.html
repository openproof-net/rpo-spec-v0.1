RPO — Rapport Probatoire Ouvert

A standard to structure, seal and preserve evidence

OpenProof defines the RPO (Rapport Probatoire Ouvert): a transparent standard combining a signed JSON structure, a human-readable PDF and a public hash. The goal is not to decide truth, but to make evidence coherent, traceable and re-evaluable over time.

What problem RPO solves

In many legal, institutional or organisational contexts, narratives evolve, documents are fragmented, and interpretations shift over time. RPO introduces a stable evidentiary object: once structured and sealed, a narrative can be analysed again later without being rewritten or retroactively altered.

RPO Sandbox

The sandbox is an open, deterministic demonstrator: paste a narrative, generate a draft RPO JSON bundle, and compute a public hash. This draft bundle illustrates how a narrative can be structured, sealed and later re-evaluated without rewriting history.
