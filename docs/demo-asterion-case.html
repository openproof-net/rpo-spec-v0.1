Signal Detection

Six months before consolidation

The programme dashboard is green. Four weak signals suggest that the official representation may already be incomplete.

01 · OBSERVE

Output remains stable

But maintenance workarounds have doubled at Site North.

Source: operations log · repeated signal
02 · MAP

Skills do not move with machines

The relocation plan maps equipment, not tacit expertise.

Missing: critical-role dependency map
03 · TEST

Competing explanation

The issue may be less “resistance to change” than loss of operating knowledge.

Hypothesis remains open
04 · INTERVENE

Run a reversible test

Transfer one line with paired teams before committing the entire consolidation.

Learning value: high · reversibility: high

Official narrative

Two plants perform similar work. The newer site can absorb the older site's volume and reduce fixed costs.

Reconstructed question

Can Site South reproduce the output without the knowledge, maintenance routines and customer-specific coordination currently embedded at Site North?

Decision made possible: postpone irreversible closure; test one production transfer; document critical knowledge and customer dependencies; review the decision after 60 days.
Crisis Reconstruction

Eight months after consolidation

Delivery failures and quality incidents are now visible. The board asks who failed. TruthX first reconstructs which representation guided the original decision.

01 · LOCALIZE

Decision record

The board pack showed assets, headcount and projected savings.

Present and hashable
02 · IDENTIFY GAP

Knowledge dependency absent

No evidence shows that tacit expertise was assessed.

Documented omission · intent not inferred
03 · COMPARE

Four narratives

Employee resistance, poor execution, faulty machines or incomplete system mapping.

None accepted without evidence
04 · RECONSTRUCT

Causal mechanism

Equipment moved faster than operating knowledge, destabilizing maintenance and quality control.

Strongest current coverage · reservations remain

What is supported

  • The dependency map was incomplete.
  • Warning signals existed before the decision.
  • Knowledge transfer lagged asset transfer.

What is not established

  • Intentional concealment.
  • Individual fault.
  • That the alternative would certainly have succeeded.
Decision made possible: stabilize operations, reconstruct the original information perimeter, distinguish structural cause from individual execution and preserve the complete audit record in an RPO.
What changes between the two moments? Before the rupture, TruthX protects optionality. After the rupture, it protects the integrity of the reconstruction. The underlying method remains the same.
