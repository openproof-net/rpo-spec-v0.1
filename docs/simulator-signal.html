Test explanations before escalation.

Competing hypotheses prevent one convenient story from taking control.

H1 · Temporary execution noise

Possible, but repeated reopening and cross-unit workarounds weaken it.

H2 · Capability gap

Compatible with some workarounds; does not explain ownership ambiguity.

H3 · Governance misalignment

Best current coverage across ownership, information, incentives and escalation.

H4 · Strategic disagreement

Plausible contributor; executive interviews remain missing.

Interpretation: indicators show evidence coverage, never a percentage probability of truth.

Choose a reversible intervention.

The purpose is to learn before committing to a costly structural response.

Clarify one outcome owner

Name the person accountable for the end-to-end result, with matching decision rights.

Harmonize one critical definition

Test whether shared data removes recurring arbitration.

Align one incentive

Stop rewarding the workflow the programme is meant to replace.

Observe for 30 days

Track whether decisions stay closed and workarounds decline.

Robustness: prefer an intervention that is reversible, informative and proportional to current evidence.
