Try the RPO Sandbox — from narrative to draft proof bundle

Paste a short narrative, add a few identifiers, and generate a draft RPO JSON bundle with heuristic scores and a public hash. This page demonstrates how the RPO standard can structure a story into something verifiable.

Experimental
Deterministic
Open-source friendly
This is an open sandbox — no AI model, no registry, no legal value. It is here to show how an RPO bundle can look and behave, before plugging advanced engines or CNRS modules.

What this page does

It takes your narrative and turns it into a draft RPO bundle:

  • Computes basic metrics (length, structure, evidence density).
  • Detects simple anchors (dates, places, temporal hints).
  • Builds a minimal rpo.json object with heuristic fields.
  • Derives a public_hash from the full content.

Think of it as an RPO sandbox: the “civil code” exists, this page shows how a lightweight engine could apply it to real narratives.

How this page works

        

          This sandbox applies the minimal RPO “civil code” to your narrative.
          It does not use any AI model: everything runs locally in your browser.
        

        
    
          
  • 
            You provide a short narrative and basic identifiers (issuer, subject,
            title).
          
    • 
          
  • 
            The engine computes deterministic heuristics: sentences, evidence
            markers, anchors.
          
    • 
          
  • 
            A minimal rpo.json bundle is generated with a
            public_hash.
          
    • 
          
  • 
            The JSON is ready to be validated, hashed again, or integrated into
            your own tools.
          
    • 
        

        

          The structure mirrors the official RPO v0.1 specification, with
          additional fields marked as heuristic_scores and
          heuristic_anchors for research use.
        

      


      

        
What this engine does not do

        

          This open engine is deliberately limited. It is a demonstrator, not a
          forensic authority.
        

        
    
          
  • It does not determine truth or falsity.
    • 
          
  • 
            It does not replace legal analysis or expert
            assessment.
          
    • 
          
  • 
            It does not connect to any remote registry or
            blockchain.
          
    • 
          
  • 
            It does not embed psycho-forensic AI models in the
            open version.
          
    • 
        

        

          The goal is to make the structure of evidence transparent and testable,
          before connecting more advanced scientific engines in restricted pilots.
