Educational case · AI governance

Why is this AI programme failing?

A fictional executive committee must decide whether to continue, stop or redesign an AI programme. TruthX does not start with the answer. It reconstructs the reality on which a robust decision can be made.

Demonstration only.
This fictional, non-confidential case illustrates the TruthX method. It is not an automated decision, professional advice or a claim that software can establish truth.

The displayed question

What the committee asks is not always the question the evidence can answer.

Displayed

Should we continue investing in the AI programme?

The board sees a €2.4m programme, six pilots and weak adoption. It wants a go/stop recommendation within ten days.

Reframed

What is actually preventing operational value?

Technology, data, process ownership, incentives, governance and decision rights must be separated before judging the programme.

TruthX principle: never accept the initial narrative as the analytical perimeter.

Competing narratives

Click a narrative to inspect its evidential coverage. No narrative is accepted because it is dominant.

A · “The technology failed”

  • Supported: adoption is weak.
  • Contradicted: technical threshold was met.
  • Missing: production failure analysis.

B · “The employees resisted”

  • Supported: usage is low.
  • Contradicted: incentives favour the legacy process.
  • Missing: segmented user research.

C · “The operating model was never redesigned”

  • Supported: no process owner, fragmented data, unchanged incentives.
  • Compatible: acceptable model quality.
  • Open: full economic baseline.

D · “The use cases were poorly selected”

  • Supported: five pilots have weak recurring use.
  • Open: selection criteria and alternative portfolio.
  • Does not alone explain governance gaps.

Formal responsibility and operational control do not currently coincide.

Causal hypotheses

Indicators describe evidential coverage. They are not “truth scores”.

H1 · Model performance is inadequate

One supporting fact · one contradicting fact · two missing tests.

Coverage: limited · contradiction open

H2 · User resistance is primary

One supporting observation · one incentive contradiction · user research missing.

Coverage: limited · causal direction unresolved

H3 · Governance and operating model are misaligned

Four compatible facts · no direct contradiction · economic baseline incomplete.

Coverage: strongest current explanation · reservation remains

H4 · Portfolio selection is poor

Two compatible facts · selection documentation missing · partial explanation.

Coverage: plausible contributor · not sufficient alone
Current model: the programme is being managed as a technology portfolio while value depends on business-process ownership, shared data and aligned incentives.

Decision options

TruthX makes the decision space explicit. The human decision-maker remains accountable.

A · Continue unchanged

Fastest politically, but preserves the structural causes of weak adoption.

Exposure: high · reversibility: medium

B · Stop the programme

Stops expenditure, but may discard viable capabilities without testing the operating-model hypothesis.

Exposure: medium · information loss: high
ROBUST NEXT STEP

C · Reframe and stage-gate

Pause new pilots; appoint one process owner; harmonize one critical dataset; align incentives; retest one use case for 90 days.

Exposure: controlled · learning value: high · reversible

D · Do nothing for 90 days

A valid counterfactual that reveals ongoing cost, political drift and opportunity loss.

Exposure: rising · reversibility: declining
